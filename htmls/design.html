My Designs

I studied Mechanical Engineering and have a strong interest in applying my knowledge to real-world projects in industry. One of the ways I enjoy doing this is through 3D design in my free time. I’m passionate about hands-on work and have created a variety of objects using SolidWorks, which I’ve brought to life using 3D printers at the University of Auckland. On this page, I’ll share some of my designs—let’s explore them together.

The CNC carved logo of The University of Auckland

I have designed a logo for The University of Auckland that can be carved onto a wooden sheet using a CNC machine. I created the 3D model using SolidWorks CAD software. Then, using a small CNC machine at the University of Auckland's Unleash Space, I carved the design onto a wooden sheet. Afterward, I painted the piece using small brushes to complete its final appearance. I'll explain the processes below:
  1. I created the 3D file in Solidworks.
  2. The file has been saved as a STL format and transfered to the PC connected to the CNC machine.
  3. At first, a thick matt has been used to carve the coarse shape.
  4. In the next step, a smaller and thinner matt has been used to carve the fine curves out of the wood.
  5. The wooden piece has been cut using a saw into a rectangular frame.
  6. The carved wood has been painted with brush and acrylic colors.
